Its in the Details

At Disney, attention to detail is something of an art form, stemming from our founder, Walt Disney. In our book, Be Our Guest, entire passages talk about this passion, which one could argue is at the very core of our company’s values: “Walt was famous for his eye for detail, and he made sure that everyone paid the same attention that he did.”

  • What does this mean to you?
  • What does this make you think of?
  • Where do we expect details today?

Actions

  • Show up on time looking and acting Professional

    • Sounds simple but you’d be surprised.
    • Don’t be that intern who shows up late in the same clothes you wore yesterday. (we’ll talk dress a little later).​

  • Communicate, Communicate, Communicate

  • Be gracious. Say thank you, a lot. ​

  • This is a workplace, don’t act like the office is your home.

    • What do I mean by that?
  • You’re working in an open space.
    • What’s a bad idea? ​
    • Personal phone calls​
    • Music or other sound from your machine that the group isn’t seeing.​
    • Loud conversation​
    • Possibly eating – smelly food, noisy chewing. ​
    • Conference call participation unless you have a headset and are just listening. ​
    • Your company has a kitchen and free coffee/snacks. What’s a good idea? ​

    • Do you know how dishes are handled? No? ASK.

      • In our office, you wash your own. You don’t leave them in the sink. FIND OUT and comply.
      ​
    • You spilled a little coffee on the counter. Do you wipe it up or let is dry? ​
    • Do you know how and when the fridge gets cleaned out? ASK ​
    • Good rule of thumb – you’ve heard of leave no trace on the hiking trail? Do the same in company kitchen. ​
    • You may be tempted to think decisions that are made and throughout your term are only impacting you. I encourage you to Think about the bigger picture. the person making the decision has a whole host of challenges you may not know about -- give benefit of the doubt. ​
    • You’ve got your own cube/desk – great. Keep it looking clean, professional and organized. Don’t leave a half empty coffee cup drying up there.​

Boy Scout Rule!

The Boy Scouts have a rule: "Always leave the campground cleaner than you found it." If you find a mess on the ground, you clean it up regardless of who might have made the mess. You intentionally improve the environment for the next group of campers. Actually the original form of that rule, written by Robert Stephenson Smyth Baden-Powell, the father of scouting, was "Try and leave this world a little better than you found it."

In every area of work, strive for this rule:
  • Dishes
  • Desk
  • Code
  • Culture!
    • Story from cracker barrel where people were getting credit for your work, then they realized every one was more productive when I was around.

    Writing

    • Don’t write like you text
    • Limit abbreviations​
    • Don’t try to add words. (There is no word count)
    • “That” - See if you can restructure to not use "That"
    • Tailor to your audience
      • Less technical?
      • More technical?
      • Executive?
      • End User?
      • Someone who knows nothing about what you’re doing?​
    • In email -- Use a salutation at least for the first email or reply in a conversation
    • Use a closing – regards, best
